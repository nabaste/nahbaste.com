About Me

A Graphic Designer turned XR Developer

While studying graphic design at University of Buenos Aires, I was always looking for new ways in which design met technology. I got into UI and UX, interested in designing interactions. But somehow designing interfaces wasn’t enough. I learned to code in order to make websites, and was thrilled to work with react designing PWAs. I later discovered OpenFrameworks and got into C and GLSL. I love the way in which you can create procedural images with code, the elegant simplicity of it. I’m a self-taught programmer, I always enjoyed learning how things work, taking them apart and playing with the pieces. Some time later I got into 3D modeling with Maya, I took an online course on hard surface modeling and I got the hang of it pretty fast. I learned some organic sculpting as well in Zbrush. And then one day last year every piece of the puzzle fell into place, and I realized that AR was the place where everything I had done came together. Taking design to the 3D space, designing interactive experiences and assets.

I’m always interested in ways in which design and technology meet to tackle real world challenges. Technology has the immense power to change the lives of so many people, and that requires an ethical exercise of design and development. I love designing interactive experiences, from an interface to an AR experience. Design should empower people, help them be more comfortable & confident to achieve their goals. I think the XR space will radically change how we conceive information, both visual and conceptual. Im a big believer in decentralization, the power of blockchain technology to secure and develop public goods, while also creating consensus for common causes.
